# A Day in the Life of Jorge: Visual Scene Descriptions

---

## DOCUMENT CONTEXT

### Project Overview

**Purpose:** This document contains detailed scene descriptions for generating educational anime-style images depicting a full day in the life of Jorge, a 67-year-old Colombian coffee farmer living in rural Quindio, in the heart of Colombia's Coffee Triangle (Eje Cafetero).

**Project Goal:** Create visual learning content that helps learners (children, ESL students, people with special learning needs, etc.) identify everyday objects and associate vocabulary with realistic images showing objects in their natural context.

**Why This Approach Works:**
- Objects shown in real-world settings (coffee beans on the plant, not isolated)
- Logical sequence helps learners understand daily routines
- Character interactions with objects show purpose and usage
- Rural Colombian/agricultural vocabulary rarely covered in traditional materials
- Cultural context embedded naturally (Colombian coffee culture)
- Elderly male perspective for diversity

## Character Profile

- **Name:** Jorge
- **Age:** 67
- **Gender:** Male
- **Ethnicity:** Colombian/Latino
- **Appearance:** Weathered tan skin from decades of outdoor farmwork, short gray hair, gray mustache, wrinkled face with kind brown eyes, strong wiry build (farmer's physique), calloused hands stained from years of handling coffee, slightly stooped posture, wedding band worn thin on left ring finger
- **Height:** Average (5'7")
- **Lives:** Traditional finca (farmhouse) in rural Quindio, Colombia, on a small coffee farm (5 hectares), with wife Dona Carmen (64)
- **Family:** Adult children who visit on weekends, grandchildren in the city
- **Work:** Coffee farmer (cafetero), 45+ years of experience, owns small farm passed down through family
- **Transportation:** Jeep Willys (classic coffee region vehicle, painted colorful), also walks
- **Faith:** Catholic -- crucifix in home, small altar with Virgin Mary, prayer before meals, religious calendar on wall
- **Style:**
  - Work: Old worn jeans or sturdy khaki work pants, plaid flannel shirt (blue and white), tall green rubber boots (botas pantaneras), wide straw hat (sombrero aguadeno), machete in leather sheath on belt
  - Home: Simple comfortable clothes, alpargatas (traditional woven sandals), same pants without boots
  - Sleep: Simple white undershirt and loose cotton pants

## PART 2: EARLY MORNING KITCHEN (5:15 AM - 5:45 AM)

### Scene 016: Entering the Kitchen
**Time:** 5:15 AM -- dim pre-dawn light, warm glow from stove
**Setting:** Farmhouse kitchen doorway
**Description:** Jorge walks through the doorway into the farmhouse kitchen. The kitchen is lit by the warm orange glow of embers in the wood-burning stove (fogon de lena) -- Carmen must have banked the coals last night. The kitchen is simple and functional: a heavy wooden counter along one wall, pots and pans hanging from iron hooks on the ceiling beams, a small modern refrigerator (a recent addition, humming softly), open shelves with stacked dishes and cups, a window looking out toward the coffee plantation. The terra-cotta tile floor continues from the main room. A wooden table with four chairs sits in the center.

### Scene 017: Stoking the Wood Stove
**Time:** 5:16 AM -- warm firelight
**Setting:** Kitchen, at the stove
**Description:** Jorge crouches before the wood-burning fogon, a heavy clay-and-brick stove built into the kitchen wall. He lifts the iron grate and pushes kindling -- thin sticks of dried guadua bamboo -- onto the glowing coals. The embers brighten, and small flames lick at the new wood. His weathered face is lit orange by the fire. A stack of split firewood sits in a wooden bin beside the stove. Smoke curls upward into the blackened chimney. The fogon has two burner circles on its flat top surface, both blackened from use.

### Scene 018: Adding Larger Wood
**Time:** 5:18 AM -- growing firelight
**Setting:** Kitchen, stove close-up
**Description:** Close-up of Jorge's calloused hand placing a thicker piece of firewood onto the now-crackling flames inside the fogon. The fire catches and roars softly, heat radiating outward. Sparks float upward. His hand is steady despite the heat. The iron grate glows red at the edges. Soot and ash crust the inside of the stove. The warmth fills the kitchen on this cool mountain morning.

### Scene 019: Placing the Kettle
**Time:** 5:19 AM -- warm firelight, pre-dawn outside
**Setting:** Kitchen, stove top
**Description:** Jorge places a blackened aluminum kettle on the flat top of the wood-burning fogon, directly over the flame circle. The kettle is dented and scorched from years of daily use, its handle wrapped in wire where the original broke. Water sloshes inside as he sets it down. The fire crackles beneath. He positions the kettle precisely over the hottest spot -- a farmer's practiced knowledge.

### Scene 020: Getting the Coffee Grounds
**Time:** 5:20 AM -- kitchen lit by firelight and gray dawn
**Setting:** Kitchen, at the shelf
**Description:** Jorge reaches up to an open wooden shelf and takes down a metal canister of ground coffee. He pries off the lid with his thumb, revealing dark brown grounds inside -- coffee from his own farm, roasted and ground by Carmen. The rich, earthy aroma of Colombian coffee fills the air. Beside the canister on the shelf sit other supplies: a bag of panela (raw cane sugar blocks), a tin of salt, a jar of dried herbs. The kitchen window behind shows the sky turning from black to deep blue.

### Scene 021: Preparing the Cloth Filter
**Time:** 5:22 AM -- warming kitchen light
**Setting:** Kitchen, counter
**Description:** Jorge sets up the traditional coffee brewing apparatus on the wooden counter: a metal ring holder with a cloth filter (colador de tela) hanging from it, positioned over a blackened metal pot. The cloth filter is stained dark brown from thousands of brews, clean but permanently coffee-colored. He spoons ground coffee from the canister into the cloth filter -- three heaping spoonfuls with a tarnished metal spoon. The grounds settle into the filter's bowl shape. Steam begins to rise from the kettle on the stove behind him.

### Scene 022: Pouring Hot Water Through Filter
**Time:** 5:25 AM -- warm kitchen glow
**Setting:** Kitchen, close-up on brewing
**Description:** Jorge lifts the steaming blackened kettle from the fogon with a folded cloth to protect his hand and pours a steady stream of hot water through the cloth filter. The water hits the dark coffee grounds and begins to drip through, a rich dark brown stream falling into the metal pot below. Steam and coffee aroma billow upward. His pour is slow, circular, saturating all the grounds evenly. The dark liquid pools in the pot, thick and aromatic. This is tinto -- strong black Colombian coffee, the way it has been made on this finca for generations.

### Scene 023: Pouring First Cup of Tinto
**Time:** 5:28 AM -- warm kitchen, growing dawn outside
**Setting:** Kitchen, counter
**Description:** Jorge pours tinto from the metal pot into a small metal cup -- a pocillo, simple and handleless, slightly dented. The coffee is dark as night, steaming. He reaches for a block of panela on the shelf -- a hard block of unrefined cane sugar, golden-brown and rough-textured. With a knife, he shaves off a few pieces directly into the cup. The panela dissolves quickly in the hot liquid, sweetening the bitter coffee. This is his first cup of the day, the most important one.

### Scene 024: Drinking Tinto at the Window
**Time:** 5:30 AM -- growing dawn light through window
**Setting:** Kitchen, standing at window
**Description:** Jorge stands at the kitchen window, both hands wrapped around the warm metal pocillo, sipping his tinto. Through the window, the coffee plantation stretches out in rows on the terraced hillside, still dark and shrouded in morning mist. The Andes mountains rise in the far distance, their peaks barely visible against the deep blue pre-dawn sky. A faint orange glow lines the eastern horizon. Jorge's face is contemplative, weathered features lit from one side by the fire glow and from the other by the approaching dawn. He drinks slowly, savoring.

### Scene 025: Carmen Enters the Kitchen

**Setting:** Kitchen doorway
**Description:** Dona Carmen appears in the kitchen doorway, a 64-year-old Colombian woman with silver-gray hair pulled back in a loose bun, warm brown eyes, and a round kind face. She wears a simple cotton housedress with a floral pattern and a worn apron tied at her waist. She smiles at Jorge, says "Buenos dias, viejo," and moves toward the stove. Jorge nods, lifts his pocillo in greeting. The kitchen is warmer now, the fire burning well, the first light of dawn reaching through the window.

### Scene 026: Carmen Heating Aguapanela

The panela begins to dissolve in the heating water, turning it amber. This is aguapanela, a traditional Colombian drink -- warm sweetened water that fuels farm workers. She stirs with a wooden spoon, the panela breaking apart. Steam rises from both the coffee pot and the aguapanela pot. The kitchen fills with sweet and earthy aromas.

The arepas sizzle as they touch the hot surface. She made the dough last night. The corn cakes begin to develop golden-brown spots on their undersides. A block of white cheese (queso fresco) sits on a wooden cutting board on the counter, ready to be sliced.

The table surface is scarred and stained from decades of meals. Four mismatched wooden chairs surround it. A simple kitchen knife cuts through it cleanly. The white slices are thick, meant to be placed on the warm arepas. The cheese was likely brought from a neighbor or the local market. ### Scene 032: Drinking Aguapanela

He places it on his head, adjusts the brim. The machete at his belt shifts as he moves. Time to head out.

## PART 4: MORNING COFFEE PICKING (6:15 AM - 9:00 AM)

### Scene 044: First Pick of the Day
**Time:** 6:15 AM -- bright morning light filtering through shade trees
**Setting:** Coffee plantation, among the rows
**Description:** Jorge positions himself before a coffee plant heavy with ripe red cherries. He reaches into the branch with his right hand, fingers finding a cluster of bright red cherries among the green leaves. With a practiced twist and pull, he separates three ripe red cherries from the stem, leaving the green ones untouched. The cherries drop into his open left palm -- small, glossy, the deep crimson of perfect ripeness. He transfers them to the coco basket at his waist. The first pick of the day. The plant's dark green leaves are still wet with morning dew.

### Scene 045: Selective Picking Close-Up
**Time:** 6:20 AM -- dappled morning light
**Setting:** Coffee plant, extreme close-up
**Description:** Extreme close-up of Jorge's weathered right hand amid coffee branches. His calloused fingers -- stained reddish-brown from decades of handling coffee -- carefully select only the bright red ripe cherries, bypassing clusters of green, yellow, and orange ones that need more time. Each ripe cherry is plucked individually with a gentle twist. A cluster shows the full color spectrum: green (unripe), yellow (turning), orange (almost), bright red (ready), and a few dark burgundy (overripe). His fingernails are short, worn, dirt embedded under the edges. This selective hand-picking is what makes Colombian specialty coffee exceptional.

### Scene 046: Moving Along the Row
**Time:** 6:25 AM -- morning light, shade and sun patches

**Description:** Carmen comes through the door carrying two small cups of evening tinto and sits in the rocking chair beside Jorge. She hands him one of the metal pocillos. Fireflies begin to appear in the garden, tiny points of green light. The farm dog lies between their chairs. They exchange a few quiet words -- about the rain, the harvest, whether their daughter will call tonight. The ease of a couple married for over forty years.

### Scene 153: Drinking Evening Tinto

The coffee is from his own farm, processed with his own hands, brewed by his wife. From plant to cup, every step was his. There is a deep contentment in this that no words express.

### Scene 154: Neighbor Arriving

They shake hands warmly, pat each other on the shoulder. Don Rodrigo has come to discuss selling some dried beans to a buyer who will visit the area next week. Carmen offers to bring him coffee.

### Scene 155: Discussing Coffee Prices
**Time:** 6:25 PM -- dusk, porch light on
**Setting:** Farmhouse porch
**Description:** Jorge and Don Rodrigo sit in the rocking chairs, talking business. Jorge has pulled a handful of dried pergamino beans from a small sample bag he keeps on the porch shelf. Carmen brings a third pocillo of tinto for the neighbor. The porch light -- a single yellow bulb -- casts warm light on the two old farmers, their straw hats on the railing beside them, their conversation a mixture of market talk and neighborly gossip.

### Scene 156: Neighbor Departing
**Time:** 6:40 PM -- early night, dark
**Setting:** Farmhouse porch, view of departing figure
**Description:** Don Rodrigo rises to leave, adjusting his straw hat. He and Jorge shake hands again, agreeing to speak before the buyer arrives. "Hasta manana, Don Jorge." "Que le vaya bien, compadre." Rodrigo walks down the dark path, his cane tapping, a flashlight flickering on in his hand. Jorge watches until the light disappears around the bend. He turns to Carmen. "Good man, that Rodrigo." She nods and collects the empty cups.

### Scene 157: Dinner Preparation
**Time:** 6:45 PM -- evening, kitchen light
**Setting:** Farmhouse kitchen
**Description:** Carmen stands at the fogon, preparing dinner. The wood stove is burning well, two pots on top. She reheats rice and beans left from lunch (calentado -- a beloved Colombian tradition of reheated leftovers). On a griddle, she fries eggs -- two for Jorge, one for herself. Arepas warm on the side of the stove. Jorge enters the kitchen and sits at the wooden table, watching her work. The kitchen is lit by a single bulb hanging from the ceiling beam, casting warm yellow light. The window is dark -- night has fallen over the mountains.

### Scene 158: Frying Eggs
**Time:** 6:50 PM -- kitchen light
**Setting:** Kitchen, at the fogon, close-up
**Description:** Close-up of eggs frying in a cast-iron skillet on the fogon. Two eggs sizzle in a thin layer of oil, their whites setting and browning at the edges, yolks still runny and golden. Carmen's hand holds the skillet handle. The cast iron is blackened from decades of use. The heat from the wood fire is uneven but she knows her stove perfectly -- she tilts the pan to pool oil over the yolks, basting them. The aroma of frying eggs mixes with the wood smoke.

### Scene 159: Dinner on the Table
**Time:** 6:55 PM -- kitchen light
**Setting:** Kitchen table
**Description:** Carmen sets the dinner plates on the table: calentado -- reheated rice and beans, thick and hearty -- with two fried eggs on top of Jorge's portion, their yolks glistening. Warm arepas on a clay plate. A cup of aguapanela, warm and amber, steams beside each plate. A small bottle of aji (hot sauce) sits in the center of the table. The meal is simple -- campo food, farmer's dinner -- but deeply satisfying. Jorge picks up his fork.

### Scene 160: Breaking the Egg Yolk
**Time:** 6:57 PM -- kitchen
**Setting:** Kitchen table, close-up
**Description:** Close-up of Jorge's fork piercing the yolk of a fried egg atop the mound of calentado. The golden yolk breaks and runs in a stream over the rice and beans, coating them in rich, liquid gold. He mixes the egg into the calentado with his fork, creating a rich, satisfying combination. The beans are dark red, the rice white, the egg yellow -- a colorful plate. He scoops a large forkful and eats. His eyes close briefly. Simple. Perfect.

### Scene 161: Eating Dinner Together
**Time:** 7:00 PM -- kitchen light
**Setting:** Kitchen table
**Description:** Jorge and Carmen eat dinner together at the wooden table. The single bulb casts a warm cone of light over the table, leaving the kitchen corners in soft shadow. They eat slowly, talking between bites. Carmen mentions their son called earlier while Jorge was in the fields -- he is coming to visit this weekend with the grandchildren. Jorge's face brightens at the news. He tears his arepa and uses it to scoop up the last of the egg and calentado from his plate. The night insects have begun their chorus outside the dark window.

### Scene 162: Drinking Aguapanela
**Time:** 7:10 PM -- kitchen light
**Setting:** Kitchen table
**Description:** Jorge wraps both hands around his warm cup of aguapanela and drinks deeply. The sweet panela drink is comforting on the cool mountain evening. Carmen clears the dishes to the wash basin, stacking them quietly. Jorge leans back in his chair, the meal settling, warmth spreading through him. He watches Carmen move around the kitchen -- the same movements she has made for forty years, still efficient, still graceful. He feels a wave of gratitude he does not express in words.

### Scene 163: Moving to Main Room
**Time:** 7:15 PM -- evening, interior light
**Setting:** Farmhouse main room
**Description:** Jorge rises from the kitchen table and walks into the main room, which serves as the living area. The room is simple: a worn sofa against one wall, a wooden bench, a small side table, and most importantly, an old battery-powered radio on a shelf. A religious calendar -- the Virgin Mary for this month -- hangs on the whitewashed wall. A crucifix is mounted above the doorway. The room is lit by a single bulb. Jorge settles onto the sofa, his body grateful for the soft cushion after a day of physical labor.

### Scene 164: Turning on the Radio
**Time:** 7:18 PM -- evening
**Setting:** Main room, close-up on radio
**Description:** Jorge reaches over to the small side table and turns the dial on the old radio. It is a simple transistor radio, its brown plastic case cracked and taped, the antenna extended at an angle. Static crackles, then a voice emerges -- Caracol Radio, the Colombian national station, broadcasting the evening news. The announcer's voice fills the small room with reports from Bogota, coffee prices, weather forecasts, regional news. Jorge adjusts the dial slightly, tuning in more clearly. This is his connection to the world beyond the finca.

### Scene 165: Listening to News
**Time:** 7:20 PM -- evening
**Setting:** Main room
**Description:** Jorge sits on the sofa, legs stretched out on a low wooden footstool, listening to the radio news. His head is tilted slightly toward the radio, brow furrowed in concentration. The announcer discusses coffee export prices -- Jorge listens intently to this segment. Coffee is his livelihood, and international prices directly affect what the cooperative will pay for his beans. He nods at some piece of information, frowns at another. His hands rest on his stomach, fingers interlaced.

### Scene 166: Carmen Knitting
**Time:** 7:25 PM -- evening
**Setting:** Main room
**Description:** Carmen sits in a wooden chair across from Jorge, knitting. Her hands work the needles with practiced speed -- click, click, click -- the yarn flowing between her fingers. She is making a small sweater for a grandchild, bright yellow wool forming even rows. A ball of yarn sits in a basket at her feet. She listens to the radio too, occasionally commenting on a news item. The lamplight catches the needles as they flash. A pair of reading glasses perch on her nose. The domestic scene is timeless -- an elderly couple, each absorbed in their evening activity, together in comfortable companionship.

### Scene 167: Jorge Reading Newspaper
**Time:** 7:30 PM -- evening
**Setting:** Main room
**Description:** Jorge unfolds a newspaper -- La Cronica del Quindio, the regional paper, a few days old from his last trip to the town of Armenia. He puts on his reading glasses, heavy black frames, and holds the paper at arm's length. He reads slowly, lips moving slightly, scanning headlines about local politics, agricultural subsidies, and a piece about the upcoming coffee festival. The newspaper is creased and slightly damp from being carried in his pocket. Carmen's knitting needles continue their rhythm. The radio plays vallenato music now -- an accordion-driven melody, the folk music of Colombia.

### Scene 168: Talking About Grandchildren
**Time:** 7:40 PM -- evening
**Setting:** Main room
**Description:** Jorge sets down the newspaper and looks at Carmen. He brings up the grandchildren -- little Sofia is five now, learning to read. And Mateo, the baby, must be walking by now. His face softens when he talks about them, the weathered features gentling. Carmen smiles, her needles pausing. She tells him Sofia drew a picture of the finca last time she visited -- coffee plants and a rooster. Jorge chuckles, a low warm sound. The distance between rural Quindio and the city where his children live is small in kilometers but large in lifestyle. These weekend visits are the highlight of their month.

### Scene 169: Checking the Farm from Window
**Time:** 7:50 PM -- night
**Setting:** Main room, at the window
**Description:** Jorge rises from the sofa and walks to the window, looking out into the dark. The porch light illuminates a small circle -- the porch railing, the nearest flower pots, the edge of the path. Beyond is darkness, but Jorge knows every contour of his land in the dark. He can make out the silhouette of the beneficiadero roof, the pale rectangle of the drying patio tarps. Fireflies drift in the garden. The night insects are loud -- crickets, frogs, the distant hoot of an owl. Everything is as it should be. His farm sleeps.

**Setting:** Bedroom, at the altar

Both make the sign of the cross. Jorge prays aloud in a low murmur -- thanking God for the harvest, the rain, the health to work another day, asking blessings for their children and grandchildren. Carmen joins in on the familiar phrases. Their clasped hands, both weathered with age, are joined in the candlelight.

### Scene 171: Blowing Out the Candle

**Time:** 8:10 PM -- evening

### Scene 173: Brushing Teeth
**Time:** 8:13 PM -- evening

He brushes methodically, leaning over the basin, foam gathering at the corners of his mouth. His reflection in the small mirror shows the familiar routine. He spits, rinses, runs his tongue over his teeth. He places the toothbrush in a ceramic cup beside the basin, next to Carmen's pink one.

### Scene 174: Carmen in the Bathroom
**Time:** 8:16 PM -- evening

**Description:** Jorge passes Carmen in the narrow hallway outside the bathroom. She carries her own toiletry items -- a cotton washcloth, a jar of face cream. They exchange a brief word as they pass. He goes to the bedroom; she enters the bathroom. The domestic choreography of shared space, performed without thought, smooth from decades of repetition.

### Scene 175: Changing into Sleep Clothes
**Time:** 8:18 PM -- evening, bedroom
**Setting:** Bedroom
**Description:** Jorge changes into his sleep clothes in the bedroom -- a clean white undershirt and loose cotton pants, the same simple outfit he wakes in each morning. He folds his clean home shirt and pants onto the wooden chair, where they will wait for tomorrow evening. The wardrobe door is open, showing the neat, spare contents. The colorful cobija on the bed has been turned down by Carmen earlier. The old alarm clock on the nightstand ticks steadily.

### Scene 176: Setting the Alarm
**Time:** 8:20 PM -- evening
**Setting:** Bedroom, nightstand close-up
**Description:** Close-up of Jorge's calloused hand reaching to the old wind-up alarm clock on the wooden nightstand. He winds the key on the back with several turns -- the mechanical spring tensing inside. He checks that the alarm hand points to 4:30 -- half an hour before dawn. The clock face is yellowed with age, the numbers faded, the glass slightly cracked. It has woken him every morning for thirty years. Beside the clock, a glass of water and his reading glasses wait. The framed image of the Virgen del Carmen watches from the wall above.

### Scene 177: Checking the Window
**Time:** 8:22 PM -- evening
**Setting:** Bedroom, at the window
**Description:** Jorge walks to the small bedroom window and looks out into the darkness. The rain has stopped entirely, and the sky has cleared. Stars are appearing -- thick, brilliant, the Milky Way visible in a way that is impossible in the city. He can see the dark outline of the mountains against the star-filled sky. The air coming through the window is cool and fresh, carrying the scent of wet earth and night-blooming flowers. No light pollution for kilometers -- just the vast Colombian sky. He leaves the window cracked open for air and pulls the simple cotton curtain across.

### Scene 178: Getting into Bed
**Time:** 8:25 PM -- evening, dim light
**Setting:** Bedroom, side view
**Description:** Jorge pulls back the colorful cobija and white sheet and lowers himself into the simple wooden bed. The mattress is old but comfortable, conforming to his body in familiar ways. He pulls the sheet up to his waist and the cobija over his legs. The bed frame creaks softly as he shifts to find his position. He lies on his right side, the position he always sleeps in. The pillow is thin and flat, cotton-covered. The nightstand lamp casts a warm glow over the bed.

### Scene 179: Carmen Getting into Bed
**Time:** 8:30 PM -- evening, warm lamplight
**Setting:** Bedroom
**Description:** Carmen enters the bedroom in her nightgown -- white cotton, embroidered at the neckline, reaching her ankles. Her gray hair is unbound, falling past her shoulders, brushed smooth. She sets her reading glasses and a small prayer book on her side of the nightstand. She lifts the cobija and gets into bed beside Jorge, the bed shifting with her weight. She adjusts the pillow, pulls the blanket to her chest. The familiar routine of settling in.

### Scene 180: Carmen Reading Prayer Book
**Time:** 8:33 PM -- evening, lamplight
**Setting:** Bedroom, close-up on Carmen
**Description:** Carmen sits propped against her pillow, reading glasses on, paging through a small, well-worn prayer book. The leather cover is soft and cracked, the pages thin and yellowed. She reads silently, her lips moving occasionally with a familiar prayer. The lamplight falls across the page. Jorge lies beside her, eyes open, watching the ceiling, his mind reviewing the day -- the cherries picked, the beans fermenting, the rain, the conversation with Rodrigo. His thoughts slow as tiredness overtakes him.

### Scene 181: Jorge Turning Off the Lamp
**Time:** 8:40 PM -- evening to darkness
**Setting:** Bedroom
**Description:** Jorge reaches over to the nightstand and clicks off the small lamp. The room plunges into darkness, except for the starlight coming through the cracked window and the faintest glow of the moon. Carmen sets down her prayer book and removes her glasses in the dark, placing them on the nightstand by feel. The sheets rustle as they both settle deeper into the bed. "Buenas noches, viejo," Carmen murmurs. "Buenas noches, mija," Jorge replies. His voice is thick with approaching sleep.

### Scene 182: The Farm Sleeps
**Time:** 9:00 PM -- night, starlight
**Setting:** Bedroom, then exterior view
**Description:** Jorge and Carmen sleep in their bed under the colorful cobija, their forms still in the darkness. Through the cracked window, stars blaze in the clear mountain sky. The old alarm clock ticks on the nightstand, counting the hours until 4:30 AM. Outside, the finca is quiet: the chickens roost in their coop, the pigs sleep in their shelter, the coffee beans ferment silently in their tanks, the drying beans rest under their tarps. The mist begins to reform in the valley, a white river of fog flowing between the dark hills. Crickets and frogs sing the night song of the tropics. In a few hours, the rooster will crow, the alarm will ring, and the cycle will begin again. The land rests, and the farmer rests with it.
